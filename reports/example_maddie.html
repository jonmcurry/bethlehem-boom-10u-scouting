2–5. Diagnosis, Comps & Plan

Primary Issue(s) Identified
Issue: Steps in the bucket — front foot opens toward third base before the swing starts.
Seen in at-bats: AB 1 & 2 vs Storm (7/12), AB 1 vs Comets (7/19) — note AB 1 vs Storm was middle-middle, so the rollover there is the swing, not the pitch.
Likely cause: Front-side flinch, likely some discomfort with inside pitches or rise balls.
Effect on outcomes: Rolls over on outside pitches for weak grounders to the left side; late/deep contact reduces power.
Issue: Casts the barrel away from the body during the downswing.
Seen in at-bats: AB 1 vs Storm (7/12), AB 1 vs Comets (7/19).
Likely cause: Compensating for the early-opening front foot — hands have to reroute to get back to the ball.
Effect on outcomes: Loopy bat path, inconsistent contact quality, occasional swing-and-miss on anything up.
Reference Comp — What a Fix Looks Like
Ichiro SuzukiHands stay inside the ball on the way down — direct fix for the casting.
Jocelyn Alo (softball)Front foot lands short and closed/square, letting the hips — not the foot — do the opening. Most directly transferable cue since same rise-ball timing.

Skipping the power-hitter comps (Ruth, Bonds) here — Maddie's issue isn't a lack of rotational force, it's sequencing (foot opens before the swing starts). The fix is timing/direction, not more hip turn.

Drill Recommendations
  • Toe-tap / quiet-foot drill — front foot picks up and lands in the same spot every time, closed-to-square, before any swing thought. Isolates the stride from the swing.
  • Wall drill for hand path — stand close enough to a wall/fence that a casting bat would hit it; forces hands to stay inside on the downswing.
