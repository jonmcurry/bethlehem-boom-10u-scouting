2–5. Diagnosis, Comps & Plan

Primary Issue(s) Identified
Issue: Arms-only swing — hips and shoulders rotate together as one unit, so all bat speed comes from the arms, with a steep uppercut path.
Seen in at-bats: AB 1 vs Riverside (7/15) was a rise ball high, so the fly out is partly pitch-influenced; AB 2 was middle-middle and still resulted in a whiff — the arms-only disconnect shows up even on a hittable pitch, which is the stronger evidence of the two.
Likely cause: No hip-shoulder separation (stretch-and-fire) — everything fires at once instead of hips leading, so there's no rotational torque, and the swing compensates with a steep bat path to try to lift the ball instead.
Effect on outcomes: Weak fly balls and empty swings; power ceiling is capped until the lower half gets involved. Only 2 at-bats logged so far — see the warning above.

Reference Comp — What a Fix Looks Like
Babe RuthFull hip turn and extension for power — the classic MLB cue for an arm-only hitter.
Haylie McCleney (softball)Her own stated principle: elite hitters sequence hips → torso → shoulders → barrel from the ground up — directly names the missing piece in Ava's swing.

Not using Ted Williams here even though he's in the general reference bank — his fact-checked, documented cue is pitch-plane matching (a downswing-angle fix), not hip rotation, so he doesn't actually fit an arms-only/no-separation issue. McCleney is the better-sourced fit for this specific flaw.

Drill Recommendations
  • Hip-turn resistance-band drill — band around the hips (or a partner holding light resistance) so she feels the hips initiating rotation a beat before the arms/hands fire.
  • Two-strike "short and quiet" drill — shortens the swing and forces a flatter path, since the uppercut tends to show up worst on pitches up in the zone.
